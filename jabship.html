Jabba's Ship Revenge

 Author(s): CmdrKrud


During an attempt to bring Jabba the Hutt to justice, Jan, all your gear, and the Nava Card from your ship were captured. Now it's up to you to get your gear back, find the Nava Card and Rescue Jan. Be prepared for anything. Jabba is aware of your abilities and will be expecting a rescue attempt. In return for smuggling goods for the Empire Jabba has acquired advanced Imperial weapons. He has also enlisted the services of one or more Mandalorian Mercenaries.

Reviewed by: David Arandle


"All difficulty levels are the same - BRUTAL !! - If you can beat this mission without being laimlame, then you truly are - << THE KING OF ALL BOUNTY HUNTERS >>" - Quote from the author

Your attempt to escape has been made considerably more difficult.
Well as you will have gathered by now this is not a new mission. As such it's one for fans of the original "Jabba's Ship" level. What we have here is this: the entire level remains the same except the existing number of enemies has been added to quite considerably as has the number of power ups and weapons.

The author says all of the difficulty levels are the same - Brutal. And he is right. In the original level you started by fending off a few kell dragons, now you have the kell dragons plus several Ree Yees and Boba...sorry, a Mandalorian warrior. This time though you are given a couple of weapons to pick up to even the odds.

Through out the level you'll have to deal with not one but three Boba Fetts plus an interrogation droid with Fett logic. Also thrown in are large number of Phase 1 Dark Troopers and plenty of enemy generators for good measure.


While some thought has gone into the placement of the various types enemies there is really nothing new here. If you are a Doom fan then this is probably how you prefer your levels - hard, with plenty to shoot and lots of power ups. If you are a DF player then this will get boring fast because you will already know the level's layout thus leaving the brain totally free to work your trigger finger. Either way this is a good example of how to turn a perfectly good level into an all out shoot-em-up. I hate shoot-em-ups!
