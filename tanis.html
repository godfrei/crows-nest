Outpost Tanis Van

 Author: Rick Horack


You are to infiltrate the Outpost Tanis Van and set three sequence charges to disable the base. You will enter the base through a cargo container that will be dropped off in the hangar.

Review by: Steve Noall


When I read the description of this level, I wasn't terribly thrilled. The plot wasn't that great, but the author does a fairly good job of making the level not seem as repetitive as some other levels like this where you must plant sequence charges.

You start out in a hangar bay with a shuttle in the middle of the hangar. The hangar is nice, but there is nothing to make it stand out from other hangar bays. The textures and architecture were nice, but nothing new. The level itself wasn't challenging at all, in fact I went right through the level as if I'd already played it; but it wasn't boring either.

One thing the author does that I really hate is give you the key to a door before you even know you need it. I prefer it when you arrive at a door and find you need a key. The enemy placement throughout the mission was good, with the kind of opposition you'd expect for a base like this. Thankfully the author didn't fill the base with Dark Troopers. There are so many levels where the author just dumps loads of Dark Troopers on you, when they really don't belong there.


This was basically a fun level, but it's not something that really makes you want to play it again. There is nothing that seperates it from other levels like this, but as I mentioned above it wasn't completely boring. If you like running around planting sequence charges, then you'll like this level.
